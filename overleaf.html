Overleaf

Overleaf accounts and subscriptions are distinct, so you can keep the same Overleaf account (where you store your projects) as you move between subscriptions. You can leave and join subscriptions with the same Overleaf account as described here:
https://www.overleaf.com/learn/how-to/Overleaf_Accounts_and_Subscriptions.
Users with existing Overleaf accounts can be added to the group subscription by the administrator or manager of the group as described here:
https://www.overleaf.com/learn/how-to/Managing_a_group_subscription. Frequent questions from users about how to join a group are answered here:
https://www.overleaf.com/learn/how-to/Joining_an_Overleaf_Group_Subscription.
So there is no need to migrate projects or create new Overleaf accounts. Users can join the group subscription with their existing account, and the premium features will simply be added to all the projects that they own.
If a user has paid for their own subscription, they can cancel that before or after they join the group subscription. Individual subscriptions can be canceled from the user’s own Account>Subscription page. If their Overleaf account is temporarily without a subscription, that is not a problem – they can still access their account, projects, and collaborations. Once they join the group subscription, they will see all of the premium features added back to their Overleaf account.
